Project OverviewThis repository contains a comprehensive analysis of lead quality for a specific publisher and advertiser across multiple referring domains. The objective was to analyze the disposition of ~3,000 leads to uncover actionable insights for performance marketing optimization.  Key Business QuestionsTrend Analysis: Are there statistically significant lead quality trends (improving or declining) over time?   Driver Identification: What segments (creative type, user demographics, ad placement) are the primary drivers of "high-quality" leads?   Revenue Modeling: Can we increase lead quality by 20% (from 8.0% to 9.6%) to justify a 20% increase in CPL (from $30 to $33)?   Data Dictionary & LogicThe dataset includes lead timestamps, contact validation scores, and creative metadata.  Quality CategorizationTo quantify performance, leads were categorized into four groups based on their CallStatus:  High Quality: "Closed" (ultimate success), "EP Sent", "EP Received", and "EP Confirmed".  Low Quality: "Unable to Contact", "Invalid Profile", and "Doesn't Qualify".  Unknown: Neutral leads that neither qualify as bad nor good.  Feature EngineeringWidget Analysis: Internal creative names (WidgetName) were parsed to isolate ad size, design (e.g., yellowarrow vs. BlueMeter), and form length (1DC vs. 2DC).  Validation Scores: Analyzed AddressScore and PhoneScore (1–5 scale) to correlate name/address matching with final conversion rates.  Ad Context: Segmented by PublisherZoneName (page location) and Advertiser CampaignName (branded vs. generic forms).  Analysis MethodologyStatistical Testing: Applied significance testing to confirm that observed quality trends were not due to random variance.  Segmentation: Evaluated lead quality across geographic (State), financial (Debt Level), and technical (IP/Referral URL) dimensions.  Optimization Strategy: Developed a budget reallocation model to phase out low-performing domains and keywords in favor of high-quality drivers to hit the 9.6% quality target.  Tools UsedSQL: Data extraction and initial lead status grouping.Python/Pandas: For cleaning complex string parameters and statistical significance testing.Power BI/Excel: For trend visualization and dashboarding.
